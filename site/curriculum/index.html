Curriculum

A research-intensive doctoral program offered in two plans (48 or 72 credits by entry qualification) — a progressive path from advanced research methods to an original, defended thesis.

Programme structure

A research-intensive doctorate offered in two plans by entry qualification. In both, the credited work is the thesis; the required courses are taken on a non-credit basis.

Plan 1.1

For master's-degree holders

Total ≥ 48 credits — thesis 48 credits (979-801).

A three-year study plan.

Plan 1.2

For bachelor's-degree holders

Total ≥ 72 credits — thesis 72 credits (979-802).

A four-year study plan.

The doctoral path

Students build advanced research methodology and disciplinary depth, pass a qualifying examination to become eligible to undertake the thesis, then carry out original research culminating in a thesis and a public oral defense — developing new environmental knowledge or innovation under the guidance of the thesis-advisory committee.

Module descriptions

Each course's description and course-level learning outcomes, as published in the official TQF2 (English text, cleaned of table-conversion artifacts — wording preserved, not paraphrased). The three required courses are taken on a non-credit basis; the credited work is the thesis.

979-701

Advanced Research Methodology

3((2)-2-5) · Required, non-credit

Advanced methods and approaches in selecting research topics and tools in environmental science, technology and management; information search for literature review or previous research; experimental and research design; preparation and presentation of the proposal; collecting and analysis of data; research quality and ethics in research; report writing and presentation.

Course learning outcomes

  1. Disseminate information, news and academic articles in the field of environmental technology.
  2. Apply research processes and techniques related to technology and solutions to environmental problems appropriately and effectively.
  3. Develop and disseminate up-to-date and reliable knowledge in the fields of technology and environmental management.
979-702

Advanced Environmental Data Analytics

1((1)-0-2) · Required, non-credit

Principles of information; advanced technologies for collecting, storing, accessing, displaying and analyzing data; the use of advanced statistical and mathematical tools to analyze environmental data in various fields; advanced environmental modeling; machine learning; geographic information systems; decision support systems; creating reports and using the results to guide environmental-management planning.

Course learning outcomes

  1. Analyze environmental data sets to identify patterns and trends, demonstrating proficiency in data mining and statistical techniques.
  2. Evaluate the impact of human activities on the environment by applying advanced modeling and simulation methods, showcasing critical thinking and problem-solving skills.
  3. Synthesize and communicate complex environmental data and analysis results effectively to diverse stakeholders, demonstrating advanced communication and presentation abilities.
979-703

Seminar

1((1)-0-2) · Required, non-credit

Environmental literature review for presentation; discussion under the supervision of experts; presentation of the research proposal and progress; applications of multimedia technology in the communication of scientific data for enhancing public awareness and understanding.

Course learning outcomes

  1. Discuss the selected environmental scientific research.
  2. Apply multimedia technology for the communication of scientific data.
  3. Apply the concept of environmental research to thesis work.
979-801 / 979-802

Thesis

48(0-144-0) Plan 1.1 · 72(0-216-0) Plan 1.2

Study, design and development of research topics related to science, technology and environmental management under the guidance of the thesis-advisory committee; developing the research to create innovation or new knowledge in environmental fields.

Course learning outcomes

  1. Analyze knowledge principles in the fields of technology and environmental management correctly.
  2. Disseminate information, news and academic articles in the field of modern environmental technology.
  3. Apply research processes and techniques related to technology and solutions to environmental problems appropriately and effectively.
  4. Systematically discuss the relationship between knowledge in the field of technology and environmental management and knowledge in other related fields.
  5. Develop and disseminate up-to-date and reliable knowledge in the fields of technology and environmental management.

Plan 1.1 (979-801) and Plan 1.2 (979-802) share the same thesis description and learning outcomes, differing in credit weight by entry qualification.

Assessment

How you are assessed

Assessment is constructively aligned to the programme's learning outcomes — what is taught, how it is learned, and how it is assessed are designed together. For a research doctorate this spans coursework, the qualifying examination, and the dissertation and defense.

Coursework

The three required courses (979-701/702/703) are taken on a non-credit basis and assessed against their course-level learning outcomes.

Qualifying examination

Passed to become eligible to undertake the thesis.

Dissertation & defense

Assessed in a public oral defense by internal and external examiners; requires at least two international journal articles, or one article plus an eligible innovation, creative work, or patent.

Grading follows the PSU Graduate Studies Regulations B.E. 2563.

Quality assurance

Programme quality

The programme admitted its first cohort in August 2024. Its curriculum, learning outcomes, and graduation requirements are governed by the official Thai Qualifications Framework (TQF2).
