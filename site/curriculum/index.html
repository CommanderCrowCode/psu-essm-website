Curriculum

A 48-credit, research-intensive doctoral program completed over three years — built as a progressive path from foundations to original contribution.

The three-year journey

Year 1
Foundation Phase
16 credits

Build advanced disciplinary foundations and critical-synthesis skills.

Year 2
Development Phase
16 credits

Data analysis and hypothesis testing; comprehensive / qualifying examination.

Year 3
Mastery Phase
16 credits

Innovation, dissertation, and defense.

Outcomes

Program learning outcomes

What graduates will be able to do — subject-specific and transferable.

PLO 1

Generate new knowledge or innovations in environmental technology through research to support sustainable development of coastal tourism cities.

PLO 2

Propose solutions or develop policies for environmental management toward sustainable development.

PLO 3

Communicate and present academic work on environmental technology and sustainable development at the international level.

PLO 4

Demonstrate moral integrity, academic ethics, and social responsibility.

PLO 5

Collaborate effectively as leaders and team members while embracing multiculturalism.
